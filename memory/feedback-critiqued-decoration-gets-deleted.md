CORRECTED 2026-07-13 (this file previously held the opposite, wrong rule — "delete critiqued decoration" — which Evan explicitly repudiated: "Did I say get rid of the pips and the boxes and make a rectangle? No.").

On PR #824's theme swatch I failed twice in opposite directions: first treated a foreign-app mock as a literal spec (copied its generic pips/blocks/status colors), then over-rotated his critique into deletion (stripped the tile to a bare "Aa" chip). Both rejected. His framing: "I gave you an example for reference. Not a spec. Use inspiration to implement something in the same spirit."

**Why:** a critique of an element ("these colors don't represent the theme", "why six boxes?") targets the element's *failure* — wrong colors, arbitrary count, wrong size — not its existence. The mock's job is the affordance and feel.

**How to apply:** extract the stated requirements (want/don't-want list) and redesign each element to satisfy them in the mock's spirit; never swing between literal copy and total strip. For the swatch, his list was: a swatch; represents the theme via its key colors; visually interesting; accommodates System later; compact; per-mode colors that differ between tiles.
