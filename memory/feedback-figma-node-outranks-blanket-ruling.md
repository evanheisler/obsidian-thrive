Evan (2026-07-17), after I held two fontSize:15 sites and asked whether his earlier "body 14 for all" ruling or their Figma nodes should win: "You gave me misleading information when I said use 14px. Now you have the info — use the fucking info! Use the figma node spec."

**Why:** His blanket ruling was made before the Figma audit existed — it was a fallback under missing information, not a standing override. Once a node spec is in hand, the lockstep doctrine already answers the question (run the design spec); re-asking makes him re-adjudicate something the project's own rules decide, and frames his stale fallback as competing authority.

**How to apply:** Figma node spec > any earlier verbal/blanket ruling made without it. When new evidence supersedes an old instruction's premise, apply the doctrine to the new evidence and report the outcome — don't present old-ruling-vs-new-evidence as a decision for Evan. Sites with no node fall back to the most recent applicable ruling. Related: [[feedback-role-first-before-design-gap]], [[feedback-resolve-framing-dont-confirm-it]].
