When declining a reviewer's suggestion, own the decision. Ground it in "following what BH-XXXX specced" or "leaving as-is" plus the concrete reason. Never frame it as another party's call ("that's a product copy decision", "defer to product") — "not my problem" is never an acceptable response; it's a cop-out that dodges ownership.

**Why:** a pushback that punts the decision reads as evasion and leaves the thread unresolved-in-spirit; owning it (spec says X, so kept X) is a real answer the reviewer can accept or contest.

**How to apply:** in bot/human review replies, say "Leaving as-is — matches the copy BH-XXXX specified, and <reason it reads fine>." Not "adding this is a product decision beyond scope." Relates to [[feedback-dont-reframe-asks-as-my-choices]] and [[feedback-resolve-framing-dont-confirm-it]].
