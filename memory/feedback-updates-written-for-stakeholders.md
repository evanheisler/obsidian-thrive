A Linear project update's audience is Evan's manager/stakeholders, not Evan. An issue-state inventory (buckets, identifiers, canceled items) is worthless to them — canceled work is never mentioned.

**Why:** A snapshot of issue states gives no insight into success, timeline, or health — the three questions the update exists to answer (2026-07-17, first /project-status-update run).

**How to apply:** Draft bodies as stakeholder narrative: where the project stands, what shipped (substance, not ticket IDs as the payload), what remains and the pace/timeline implication, and gates that threaten the path. Omit canceled/superseded issues entirely. The reality-check step with Evan stays terse and internal; the draft itself never addresses him. Related: [[feedback-report-outcomes-not-plumbing]].
