When grilling/planning, do NOT spend a question asking Evan to confirm your own
framing or mental model ("is this two-axis reading right?", "confirm type = brand,
color = mode?"). He found this confusing and said "it's not how I think" — the
framing is mine to settle.

**Why:** A confirm-my-model question wastes his turn and offloads work he's relying
on me to do. His decisions are genuine forks — scope, product priority, API shape he
opened, deliverable boundary — not "did I understand the architecture."

**How to apply:** Resolve technical/domain framing myself and state it as settled
context. Reserve questions for choices that are actually his (cost/risk/priority,
things he explicitly opened). If I'd learn the answer by thinking harder or reading
code, it's not a question. Related: [[feedback-mirror-users-model-verbatim]],
[[feedback-correction-is-not-a-go-signal]], [[feedback-dont-reframe-asks-as-my-choices]].
